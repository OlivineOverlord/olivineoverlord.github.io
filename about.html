I am a postdoctoral researcher in Earth Sciences at the University of Cambridge. My work focuses on mantle melting, carbon cycling, and planetary differentiation, with research that spans Earth and Mars. I combine experimental petrology, geochemical analysis, and theoretical modelling to understand how planetary interiors evolve.

I investigate how volatiles and trace elements are stored, mobilised, and concentrated during mantle melting, using melt inclusions, stable isotopes, high-pressure experiments, and thermodynamic modelling. My research connects mantle source heterogeneity to magma chemistry and surface mineral systems — from the Earth's lithosphere to the Martian mantle.
